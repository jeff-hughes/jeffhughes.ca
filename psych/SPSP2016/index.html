SPSP 2016 Conference Poster:
Jeffrey Hughes, James W. Beck, & Abigail A. Scholer

When Slowing Down Gets You Down: The Effect of Velocity on Frustration Over and Above Expectancy


Click to download poster

Jeffrey Hughes (j4hughes@uwaterloo.ca)

Jeff is in his fifth year of the social psychology graduate program at the University of Waterloo. His research lies at the intersection of motivation and self-regulation, group dynamics, and decision-making. With Abigail Scholer, he is currently investigating how motivational orientations like regulatory focus and regulatory mode can influence whether and how individuals maximize when making decisions. He is also examining the role of regulatory focus in how people shift in the way they evaluate and organize their self-concept.

James Beck (james.beck@uwaterloo.ca)

James' research deals primarily with motivational and self-regulatory processes. He is interested in how individuals allocate finite resources, such as time, effort, and attention across multiple, competing demands. Much of his work to date has dealt with how individuals use beliefs about the likelihood of successfully attaining their goals to efficiently allocate finite resources.

Abigail Scholer (ascholer@uwaterloo.ca)

Abby received her Ph.D. in social psychology from Columbia University in 2009. She was on the faculty of her undergraduate alma mater, Gettysburg College, for two years before joining the faculty of University of Waterloo in 2011. Her research interests relate to the study of self-regulation, motivation, and social cognition, as broadly described on her lab website.
