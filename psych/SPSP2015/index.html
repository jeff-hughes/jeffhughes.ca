SPSP 2015 Conference Poster:
Jeffrey Hughes, Igor Grossmann, & Adam B. Cohen

Tolerating the “Doubting Thomas”: How Emphasis of Religious Beliefs versus Practices Influences Anti-Atheist Prejudice


Click to download poster

Jeffrey Hughes (j4hughes@uwaterloo.ca)

Jeff is in his fourth year of the social psychology graduate program at the University of Waterloo. His research lies at the intersection of motivation and self-regulation, group dynamics, and decision-making. With Abigail Scholer, he is currently investigating how motivational orientations like regulatory focus and regulatory mode can influence whether and how individuals maximize when making decisions. He is also examining the role of regulatory focus in how people shift in the way they evaluate and organize their self-concept.

Igor Grossmann (igrossmann@uwaterloo.ca)

Igor is an assistant professor of psychology at the University of Waterloo. His research interests revolve around two issues. First, to understand the processes that enable individuals to think and act “wisely,” for instance by using cognitive strategies that facilitate the resolution of social conflicts or by regulating emotions that undermine their goals and compromise their health. Second, to understand how culture shapes cognition and emotion and how culture evolves, given the mutual constitution of culture and the mind.

Adam Cohen (adamcohen@asu.edu)

Adam is an associate professor of psychology at Arizona State University. His main research interests fuse cultural, social, and personality psychology. He is interested in how religious differences function as cultural differences, affecting domains including religious identity and motivation, well-being, moral judgment, forgiveness, and the like. He is also interested in applying evolutionary theory to religion.
