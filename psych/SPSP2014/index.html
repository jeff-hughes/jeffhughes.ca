Simply the Best: Using Regulatory Focus and Mode to Reconceptualize Maximization

Jeffrey Hughes (j4hughes@uwaterloo.ca)

Jeff is in his third year of the social psychology graduate program at the University of Waterloo. His research lies at the intersection of motivation and self-regulation, group dynamics, and decision-making. With Abigail Scholer and Richard Eibach, he is currently investigating questions regarding how individuals persist on or substitute goals and means after failure. He is also examining how motivational processes may shed light on various adaptive or maladaptive forms of maximization.

Abigail Scholer (ascholer@uwaterloo.ca)

Abby received her Ph.D. in social psychology from Columbia University in 2009. She was on the faculty of her undergraduate alma mater, Gettysburg College, for two years before joining the faculty of University of Waterloo in 2011. Her research interests relate to the study of self-regulation, motivation, and social cognition, as broadly described on her lab website.
